Executive Summary

A data-driven, emotionally intelligent marketing campaign designed to position MathEtc as the go-to academic intervention during Maryland's most anxious parenting moment: March report card season.

The Opportunity

March 15

Report cards drop across Maryland school districts, creating a 4-week window of peak parent anxiety and decision-making urgency.

The Approach

3 Phases

Meet families at every emotional stage: Anticipation (pre-report), Crisis (D-Day), and Recovery (post-report action).

The Format

Cinematic

Authentic storytelling via young content creators (25-28) speaking directly to parents and students. Studio-shot, multi-angle, emotionally resonant.

The Market

12 Cities

Baltimore, Silver Spring, Rockville, Bethesda, Columbia, Towson, and surrounding Maryland metro areas. In-person + online delivery.

Core Strategic Insight

Parents don't just need tutoring—they need peace of mind. Students don't just need better grades—they need confidence restoration. Report card season is the perfect storm: high emotion + high intent + clear timeline. We're not selling sessions. We're selling relief from anxiety and a roadmap out of academic struggle.

The Customer Journey Map

Three distinct emotional phases requiring tailored messaging and content.

1
EARLY MARCH • WEEK 1-2

Anticipation Phase

Emotional State: Parents feel gut instinct something's wrong. Students silently drowning under pressure but hiding it.
Pain Points:
  • "I think my child is struggling but should I wait?"
  • "I'm behind but maybe I can pull it off"
  • Fear of being "that parent" vs. finding out too late
MathEtc Solution: Early intervention catches problems before they become disasters. Trust your instincts. Act while there's time.
Content Released:
  • Video 1: "That Feeling You're Ignoring"
  • Video 2: "The Pressure They Won't Tell You About"
2
MID MARCH • WEEK 3

D-Day Phase

Emotional State: Shock, disappointment, guilt, panic. The grades are in and they're worse than expected.
Pain Points:
  • "The grades are worse than I thought. What NOW?"
  • "Everyone's disappointed. I feel stupid."
  • "How did I miss this? Is it too late?"
MathEtc Solution: This report card hurts, but it's not the final word. Diagnostic tutoring identifies the root breakdown. 6-8 weeks is enough to create change.
Content Released:
  • Video 3: "When the Report Card Is Worse Than Expected"
  • Video 4: "If You're a Student Watching This..."

LATE MARCH • WEEK 4

Recovery Phase

Emotional State: Motivated, determined, ready to act BUT overwhelmed and unsure where to start.
Pain Points:
  • "I want to help but don't know where to start"
  • "Which tutoring works? How do I know?"
  • "I'm too far behind to catch up"
MathEtc Solution: Structured recovery plans. Custom learning paths. Progress tracking. Next term doesn't have to be a repeat IF you start now.
Content Released:
  • Video 5: "What to Actually Do After a Bad Report Card"

Production Notes

  • Format: Talking head with 2 consistent angles (MCU + wider/profile)
  • Set: Studio living room corner—couch, side table, warm lighting, relatable not clinical
  • Talent: Young content creators (25-28 yrs old), authentic not overly polished
  • Secondary Clips: Close-ups of report cards, textbooks, phones, study materials—atmospheric not literal
  • Tone: Empathetic, confident, action-oriented—emotional but not manipulative
  • Music: Minimal. Ambient tones. Let silence breathe. Slightly warmer in Phase 3.
